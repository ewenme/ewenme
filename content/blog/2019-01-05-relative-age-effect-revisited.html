This phenomenon is known as relative age effect (RAE). Participation in a given field is higher amongst those born closer to a cut-off date used to determine age cohorts for said field. One common example of a cut-off date is the academic year - two children born two days apart, but on either side of a school year cut-off date, will be allocated to different school cohorts. Furthermore, one of these kids will be possibly the oldest child relative to their peers, while the other could be the youngest of theirs.
